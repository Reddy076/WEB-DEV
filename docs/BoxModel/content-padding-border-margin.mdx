# Content, Padding, Border, Margin

A detailed breakdown of the properties that control the Box Model.

## 1. Margin (Outer Spacing)
Creates space **between** elements.
- **Shorthand**: `margin: 10px 20px 10px 20px` (Top Right Bottom Left - Clockwise).
- **Auto**: `margin: 0 auto` centers a block horizontally.
- **Negative**: `margin-top: -10px` pulls element up.
- **Collapse**: Adjacent vertical margins merge to the largest one.

## 2. Padding (Inner Spacing)
Creates space **inside** the element.
- **Shorthand**: Same as margin (Clockwise).
- **No Negative**: Cannot have negative padding.
- Used to give content "breathing room".

## 3. Border (The Line)
- `border-width`: `1px`.
- `border-style`: `solid`, `dashed`, `dotted`, `none`.
- `border-color`: `red`.
- **Shorthand**: `border: 1px solid red;`

## 4. Content
Controlled by `width` and `height`.
- `min-width` / `max-width`: Limits.
- `min-height` / `max-height`.

## 6. Important Interview Questions

<details>
  <summary>**1. What is Margin Collapse?**</summary>
  When vertical margins of adjacent block elements meet, they combine into a single margin equal to the larger of the two values.
</details>

<details>
  <summary>**2. How to "un-collapse" margins?**</summary>
  Put something between them (like `padding: 1px` or a `border` on the parent container) or use a BFC (Block Formatting Context) via `overflow: hidden`.
</details>

<details>
  <summary>**3. Margin vs Padding: When to use which?**</summary>
  - Use **Margin** to separate this element from neighbors.
  - Use **Padding** to separate content from the border of this element.
  - *Tip: If you need a clickable background area, use padding.*
</details>

<details>
  <summary>**4. Can margin be percentage based?**</summary>
  Yes, but vertical margin % is calculated based on the **width** of the container, not the height.
</details>

<details>
  <summary>**5. How to draw a circle?**</summary>
  Square width/height + `border-radius: 50%`.
</details>
